My dad kept a bald eagle mug on his workbench for as long as I can remember. The handle was chipped and the paint had gone soft from a thousand dishwasher cycles. When he passed, my mom asked if I wanted it. I said yes before she finished the sentence. That mug sits on my desk now, and every time I pick it up I am reminded that wildlife merch is rarely just about the animal. It is usually about the person you inherited it from or the person you are shopping for.

The bald eagle recovery is one of those quiet conservation wins people tend to forget. In 1963 there were only 417 known nesting pairs in the lower 48 states. Today there are roughly 316,700 birds. That history is worth knowing when you hand someone an eagle gift.
