## The Spring Arrival Ritual

Ruby-throats start arriving in the southern US in mid-February and push north through April and May. Early March at the Gulf Coast. Late April in Ohio and southern New England. Mid-May in Maine, Minnesota, and Quebec. Hummingbird Central maintains a migration map with real-time sightings submitted by watchers across North America.

Here is the actual insight about gifting. A hummingbird gift is most appreciated if it arrives RIGHT before they see the first bird of the season, not after. Once the feeders are up and the birds are back, the gift becomes a nice-to-have. Before the first sighting, it is a countdown marker. It is the thing she drinks her coffee out of the morning she sees the first one of the year and texts you about it.

Aim to have gifts arrive two weeks before their historical first-sighting date. For the south, late January. Mid-Atlantic, late March. Northern tier, mid-April. Mother's Day lands early May, perfect for roughly half the country and slightly late for the other half, which is fine because Mother's Day is an occasion, not a calendar optimization. Worth knowing: ruby-throats migrate up to 2,000 miles from eastern North America to Central America, including a non-stop 500-mile flight across the Gulf of Mexico fueled by body fat doubled before departure (Audubon Society). Drop that in the card.

There are two kinds of hummingbird merch and they are for two different people. Knowing which one to buy is the whole game.

The accurate camp includes the Ruby Throated Hummingbird Birding Tee and the Critter Patch with the host plants and the Portmeirion tankard. These are the ones a real birder can hold up and go "yes, that is a ruby-throated, the throat patch is correct, the proportions are right, the bird is not stretched." The stylized camp includes the Harvest Green Studio mugs and the Onebttl tumbler and the Hummingbird Over Purple Flowers mug. These are not wrong, they are just not trying to be field guides. They are trying to be pretty. The hummingbird is an art element, not a data point. The person who wants a stylized mug does not care whether the bird is a ruby-throated or an Anna's or a cartoon. They care whether it looks nice in the cabinet and whether it feels good in the hand.

Both groups are real. Do not judge either. The mistake is buying a stylized mug for a birder or an accurate field-guide tee for the mom who just thinks they are pretty. Match the merch to the person.

## What NOT to Buy

Three categories to avoid entirely. No affiliate links in this section on purpose.

First, red-dye hummingbird nectar. The red dye is unnecessary, the feeder itself already has red plastic on it which is all the attraction they need, and there is ongoing veterinary concern about dye in the nectar. Plain white sugar dissolved in water at a 1 to 4 ratio is the correct recipe and it is what every rehabber and every ornithologist recommends. One cup sugar, four cups water, boil to dissolve, cool, fill. That is it.

Second, feeders with plastic bee guards. The guards are supposed to keep bees and wasps out. What they actually do is trap bees and wasps and kill them slowly. Get a saucer-style feeder without bee guards. The hummingbirds still feed, the bees do not get stuck, nothing dies.

Third, "realistic" bird figurines that are the wrong species painted to look like the right one. This one is specific but the mass-market figurine industry regularly ships a generic songbird body painted with ruby-throat colors, and the proportions are always off. A birder can tell from across the room and it will bug them forever. If you want a figurine, buy from a wildlife art specialist or skip it.

### When do hummingbirds return to my state?

Ruby-throats hit the Gulf Coast in mid-February, the mid-Atlantic by late March, the Great Lakes and New England by late April, and the northernmost reaches of their range by mid-May. Anna's hummingbirds are year-round residents on the Pacific coast and have actually expanded their range northward into British Columbia in recent decades (Cornell Lab of Ornithology). The Hummingbird Central migration map tracks real-time first-sighting reports by zip code.

### Why does species accuracy matter on hummingbird merch?

Because 360 hummingbird species exist and a serious birder can ID the one in their yard by sight. A generic silhouette shirt could be any of them. A tee that names "Ruby Throated" commits to the bird they actually see, which turns the shirt into an identity marker instead of a novelty print. For casual fans who just like hummingbirds, accuracy does not matter. For anyone who keeps a list, it matters a lot.

### What do hummingbirds symbolize?

Across cultures in the Americas they symbolize joy, resilience, and the return of spring. The Aztec god Huitzilopochtli, the god of war and the sun, was depicted as a hummingbird, and Aztec warriors believed slain warriors became hummingbirds in the afterlife (Smithsonian National Museum of the American Indian). In modern North American folklore they frequently appear as messengers from deceased loved ones, especially mothers and grandmothers, which is part of why hummingbird gifts carry so much memorial weight.
