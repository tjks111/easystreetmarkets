## Understanding the Different Subspecies

When you are shopping for a true tiger fan, it helps to know a little bit about the animals themselves. Not all tigers are the same, and someone who loves them might have a favorite subspecies.

The Bengal tiger is the most numerous and the one you most commonly see in photographs. They are primarily found in India and have that iconic, bright orange coat with dark black stripes. Gifts featuring Bengal tigers are usually very vibrant and energetic.

The Siberian tiger, also known as the Amur tiger, is the largest of the big cats. They live in the freezing climates of the Russian Far East. Because of their environment, they have thicker fur and a paler orange coat. A gift featuring a Siberian tiger often has a more rugged, wintery feel to it.

Then there is the Sumatran tiger, which is the smallest surviving subspecies. Found only on the Indonesian island of Sumatra, they have much darker, closer-set stripes. A fan who loves Sumatran tigers is usually deeply invested in conservation, as they are critically endangered.

Taking the time to notice which type of tiger your friend or family member prefers can turn a good gift into a great one. It shows that you actually listen when they talk about their favorite animals.
